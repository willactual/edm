Emotional design uses common ways in which emotion and learning processes interact to foster specific learning outcomes.

color, shape, expression, etc.

Applying positive emotional design principles to learning materials can facilitate increases in cognitive processes and learning.

Three levels of cognitive responses in design:

Visceral—first impressions of the design.

Behavioral—Users subconsciously evaluate how the design helps them achieve goals, and how easily.

Reflective—consciously judge design performance and benefits.
